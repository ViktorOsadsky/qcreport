What is QCReport?
QCReport is a novel approach to radiology quality control. Specifically designed for filtering chest x-ray datasets, QCReport removes undiagnosable images that may affect AI training results.
Many CXR datasets (publicly available or privately sourced) have a problem. Some images that are completely undiagnosable due to quality decrease AI training accuracy. While it is true that challenging images help models tackle these situations when deployed, cases that have no possible information for diagnosis decrease prediction accuracy and cause model confusion.
Why QCReport?
Most proposed radiograph quality scoring systems follow a systematic approach, or simply predicting if an image has technical limitations by identifying artifacts, rotation, penetration, etc. QCReport is far more comprehensive. By training on the cases that radiologists reject, aspects such as severe pathologies or film quality would also be included in model predictions. Additionally, QCReport follows a novel scoring system.
How it Works
The QCReport scoring system allows for greater freedom to the user. Each case is scored on a scale of 0 to 9, with 0 signifying a completely undiagnosable image, and 9 signifying a good quality image. The user can set their own threshold quality limit to their dataset, and experiment until they reach their best results.

Cut-Off scores influence the severity of quality scoring. For example, if a customer specifically requests only significantly high quality chest radiographs, a cut-off score of 7 would exclude every image with a lower score predicted by QCReport.
1.
Set A Custom Cut-Off Score
Scores are continuous values. Generally, a cut-off score of 3 yields the best results.
By passing data to an external server, QCReport scores each image from 0 to 9.
2.
Run QCReport on Customer CXR Dataset
QCReport is much faster and far more efficient than human radiologist quality graders.
After receiving a quality score for every image, exclude the lowest scores possible while still making a noticeable change. While scores above 3 to 5 are low quality, they are still diagnosable. These challenging images are good to have in a dataset, as they expose the model to real-world data.
3.
Exclude or Flag Images Under Cut-Off Score
For high-quality datasets, the number of flagged images would normally be low. However, even for these, QCReport can provide improvement.
Datasets processed have no fixed limit size. However, there is a fee based on the amount of images to assess.
1.
Receive Dataset Quality Scores from QCReport
Scores are continuous values. Dataset scores will returned in one document containing the file name and associated score.
Some datasets may require unique cut-off scores for the best results. While time consuming, re-training on multiple cutoffs will provide best results for any dataset.
2.
Test Multiple Cut-Off Points for Best Results
Ensure that convergence testing is performed to pinpoint the best possible cut-off.
While rare, some datasets do already exclude low-quality images. Therefore, while still useful on this sort of data, QCReport works best with raw radiological chest radiographs taken directly from a hospital archive.
3.
Exclude Images Under Cut-Off Score
After identifying the most effective cut-off score, the final model trains with better results (if any poor quality is present).
While the current version of QCReport generates predictions based on what is best for AI training data, it also has use for other statistical research. For example, it can evaluate the quality of radiographs taken at specific hospitals or clinics for quality reports.
QCReport for Non-Annotation Purposes
QCReport could serve as a valuable tool for a variety of clinical trials, data collection, or other research projects.
Even though it is still early to consider hospital integration, QCReport is open to any interest for the future. Possible hospital integration could include direct reporting within modalities, to suggest if a retake is needed before the patient leaves the hospital.
QCReport in a Clinical/Hospital Setting
Currently, QCReport does not have any approvals or clinical validation. However, hospital integration is a primary future goal.
At its current state, QCReport is being focused on radiograph annotation companies, specifically for CXR. However, as development progresses, QCReport will hopefully be able to cover all anatomical sites. Reach out if you would like to be kept updated!
Other Uses of QCReport
Due to its flexibility, QCReport can be used in a variety of ways. If this may require modifications, please feel free to ask!
